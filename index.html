Yusuf Abdi Sheikh Husen (Yusuf Ruto)

Our Manifesto

The Four Pillars of Progress

A comprehensive blueprint for the transformation of Wajir East — built on the needs of the people, grounded in action, and driven by accountable leadership.

Pillar One

Agriculture & Pastoralism

Wajir East is predominantly a semi-arid region where the majority of residents depend on pastoralism and subsistence farming for their livelihoods. Despite this reality, little investment has been directed toward the sector, leaving pastoralists exposed to the devastating effects of droughts, livestock disease, and fluctuating market prices. Yusuf Ruto recognizes that agriculture is not merely an economic activity — it is the backbone of survival, culture, and community resilience in Wajir East.

His plan is to lobby the national government for the construction of strategic water pans and boreholes across the constituency to ensure that livestock never perish due to drought. He will push for the establishment of veterinary outreach programs that bring animal health services directly to herders in remote areas, reducing livestock mortality and boosting productivity. Beyond livestock, Yusuf will advocate for irrigation schemes along seasonal rivers to enable small-scale farming of drought-resistant crops like sorghum, cowpeas, and green grams. He also plans to link farmers and pastoralists to cooperative societies and county market systems so that they can sell their produce at fair prices without exploitation by middlemen.

💧 Water Pans & Boreholes 🐄 Veterinary Services 🌾 Irrigation Schemes 🤝 Farmer Cooperatives

Pillar Two

Youth Employment & Empowerment

The youth of Wajir East represent the single greatest untapped resource in the constituency. With a rapidly growing population where over 60% are under the age of 35, unemployment remains one of the most pressing challenges facing the region. Many young men and women, despite their ambition and talent, lack access to technical skills, startup capital, and mentorship networks that would enable them to build sustainable livelihoods. This not only breeds frustration but also creates vulnerability to radicalization, crime, and migration.

Yusuf Ruto's vision is to transform this challenge into an opportunity. He commits to establishing a vocational training centre in Wajir East that offers courses in welding, electrical installation, plumbing, tailoring, ICT, and agribusiness — trades that are immediately applicable in local and regional economies. He will work with the NG-CDF fund to provide bursaries and startup grants for young entrepreneurs, and will partner with county government programs to link graduates with employment opportunities in public works projects. Additionally, he plans to create youth sports leagues, cultural groups, and digital hubs that channel youthful energy into productive, community-building activities.

🎓 Vocational Training 💼 Startup Grants 💻 Digital Hubs ⚽ Sports & Culture

Pillar Three

Infrastructure & Connectivity

Infrastructure is the foundation upon which every other form of development rests. In Wajir East, the lack of all-weather roads continues to isolate communities, especially during the rainy season when villages become completely inaccessible. This isolation drives up the cost of essential goods, prevents children from reaching school, delays patients from accessing medical care, and disconnects farmers from markets. Poor infrastructure is not just an inconvenience — it is a direct barrier to human dignity and economic growth.

Yusuf Ruto's infrastructure agenda centers on advocating for the tarmacking and grading of key road corridors that link wards within Wajir East to the main Wajir-Garissa highway and to neighbouring constituencies. He will leverage NG-CDF allocations and national government partnerships to fund the construction of culverts, bridges, and drainage systems that make rural roads usable year-round. Beyond roads, he plans to champion electrification of market centres and public institutions through the rural electrification program, as well as push for mobile network expansion to ensure no ward is left in a communication blackout. Reliable power and internet connectivity will unlock e-commerce, e-learning, and digital financial services for communities that have long been excluded.

🛣️ All-Weather Roads 🌉 Bridges & Culverts ⚡ Rural Electrification 📶 Network Expansion

Pillar Four

Education & Scholarships

Education is the most powerful tool for breaking the cycle of poverty, and yet Wajir East continues to face some of the lowest literacy rates and school completion rates in Kenya. Many primary schools lack adequate classrooms, desks, textbooks, and trained teachers. Girls face compounded barriers including early marriage, distance from school, and lack of sanitation facilities. Secondary school enrollment drops sharply as families are unable to afford fees, uniforms, and boarding costs. Without intervention, an entire generation risks being left behind.

Yusuf Ruto is deeply committed to reversing this trend through a multi-pronged education strategy. He will maximize NG-CDF allocations toward the construction and renovation of classrooms, installation of water and sanitation facilities in all public schools, and procurement of learning materials. He will establish a constituency scholarship fund targeting the brightest but neediest students at secondary and university levels, with a special quota for girls and students with disabilities. He will also advocate for the posting of more trained teachers to Wajir East schools by working with the Teachers Service Commission, and will push for the establishment of a satellite campus of a public university or technical college in the area to enable higher education access without the burden of relocation.

🏫 School Renovation 🎓 Scholarship Fund 👩‍🏫 More Teachers 🏛️ Tertiary Access
